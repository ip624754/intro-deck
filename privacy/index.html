Privacy Policy

Intro Deck is a Telegram-native professional directory and approval-based contact workflow. Active, listed profile cards may be browsed by bot users, while private contact details and private continuation remain controlled by the supported request flows.

Quick summary

What Intro Deck processes, why it is processed, and how visibility and contact access stay controlled.

Open @introdeckbot Back to homepage

1. Information we process

  • Telegram identifiers and account metadata needed to operate the bot.
  • Basic LinkedIn account identity data received through the LinkedIn authorization flow, such as name, photo, locale, email, or public profile URL when available.
  • When Verified on LinkedIn is enabled and you authorize it, category-only verification signals such as IDENTITY or WORKPLACE, the source tier, API version, and synchronization timestamps.
  • Member-provided profile information you add or edit inside the bot, such as display name, headline, company, city, about, skills, and listing status.
  • Intro request records, direct-contact state, and DM workflow state needed to run the product.
  • Operational data such as notification receipts, retry metadata, anti-abuse guards, purchase receipts, and security logs.
  • When you explicitly share a listed profile on LinkedIn, the exact post draft, publication state, provider post/request identifiers, and failure metadata needed for idempotency and support.

2. Why we process this information

  • To connect a LinkedIn account identity through sign-in. This does not verify member-entered professional claims.
  • To show an explicit LinkedIn identity or workplace verification signal when LinkedIn returns that category and the applicable product mode allows the signal to be displayed.
  • To create and maintain user profiles and directory listings inside Telegram.
  • To deliver intro requests, direct-contact requests, DM relay flows, and related decisions.
  • To keep the service secure, prevent abuse, and maintain operational reliability.
  • To diagnose failures and support audit, receipts, and service integrity workflows.
  • To publish the exact profile-share draft you reviewed only after your separate LinkedIn authorization.

3. Verified on LinkedIn data boundaries

Intro Deck stores only the category-level verification result needed for the trust feature. It does not store a LinkedIn verification URL, government identity document, verified legal name, verification method, or raw verification evidence in the Development or Lite integration. LinkedIn access tokens, refresh tokens, and ID tokens are not retained by this feature. Public badges are fail-closed: they require LinkedIn Lite mode, an explicit operator feature flag, and a fresh Lite snapshot within the configured age limit. Development snapshots remain private testing evidence and are never displayed as public badges.

4. Share on LinkedIn boundaries

Share on LinkedIn is optional and user initiated. Intro Deck shows the exact post text and visibility before opening LinkedIn authorization. Nothing is published automatically or in the background. The OAuth access token used for the approved post is not stored by Intro Deck. Intro Deck retains a minimal publication receipt and idempotency state so retries, duplicate callbacks, and uncertain provider outcomes do not silently create duplicate posts. An uncertain outcome blocks automatic retry and asks the member to check the LinkedIn feed.

5. How visibility and contact access work

Profiles are shown in the directory only when they are both active and listed. Those listed cards are visible to bot users. Member-entered professional fields are not verified by LinkedIn or Intro Deck unless explicitly stated. Contact details are not public by default, and private continuation stays controlled through intro requests, direct-contact requests, and private DM relay.

6. Data sharing

Intro Deck does not sell personal information. Data is shared only as needed to operate the service, such as with infrastructure providers, LinkedIn for sign-in, and Telegram for bot delivery. Profile and workflow information may be shown to other users only through the product surfaces that support directory discovery and contact decisions.

7. Retention and security

Intro Deck keeps operational and workflow data only for as long as reasonably needed to run the service, maintain history, support anti-abuse protections, and investigate failures. Security controls include webhook secret validation, protected operational routes, anti-abuse guards, and durable receipt/event tracking.

8. Your choices

  • You may edit your profile fields inside the bot.
  • You may control whether your profile is listed and active in the directory.
  • You may choose the supported contact mode for new requests, subject to the product rules shown in the bot.
  • You may stop using the bot at any time.

9. Contact

For privacy questions or requests related to Intro Deck, contact the operator through the official Intro Deck LinkedIn page or the official Telegram bot at @introdeckbot.

AI/news draft data

When you explicitly use AI/news drafts, Intro Deck sends your selected topic filters to NewsData.io to retrieve current article metadata. After you select one article, Intro Deck stores a minimized source evidence snapshot: title, source name, source URL, publication time, limited description/content excerpts, query snapshot, and integrity hashes.

Intro Deck sends that source evidence and a limited professional-profile context to OpenAI to generate a draft. OpenAI requests are configured with storage disabled. Intro Deck does not send LinkedIn OAuth access tokens to NewsData.io or OpenAI.

Saved preset settings, schedule state, generation runs, drafts, edits, evidence claims, model metadata, approval events, Telegram delivery receipts, and the final LinkedIn provider receipt may be stored for user review, audit, duplicate prevention, rate limiting, and support. Nothing is published automatically. Each LinkedIn post requires a separate explicit authorization through the existing one-shot publishing flow.
